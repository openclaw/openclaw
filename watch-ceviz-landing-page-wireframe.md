# Watch Ceviz, Landing Page Wireframe Draft

## Goal

Translate the final copy into a simple page structure that can be implemented quickly.

## Page structure

### Section 1, Hero

**Layout**

- Eyebrow
- Headline
- Subheadline
- 4 value bullets
- Pricing chip: `Personal — $29/mo`
- Primary CTA
- Secondary CTA
- Optional product visual: watch screen + phone screen pair

**Visual intent**
The watch and phone should appear together so the split model is obvious immediately.

---

### Section 2, What it is

**Layout**

- Short section title
- 2-3 short paragraphs
- Small contrast list:
  - not a wrist chatbot
  - not a generic assistant clone
  - yes: access layer to important agent workflows

**Visual intent**
Keep this mostly text. It is a clarity section, not a feature wall.

---

### Section 3, How it works

**Layout**
3 columns or stacked cards:

1. Watch
2. iPhone
3. OpenClaw

Each card contains:

- one short title
- one sentence
- one supporting line

**Visual intent**
Make the watch -> phone -> backend flow feel intentional and simple.

---

### Section 4, Why this matters

**Layout**

- Section title
- short paragraph
- 4 pain/value bullets

**Visual intent**
Founder-operator resonance. This should read like lived reality, not marketing fluff.

---

### Section 5, What Personal includes

**Layout**

- section title
- short framing line
- feature list
- small note: `Built for fast awareness, not deep operator control.`

**Visual intent**
This section should make the public plan legible without bringing Pro into the page too early.

---

### Section 6, Example workflows

**Layout**
3 scenario cards:

- Deploy check
- PR snapshot
- Incident triage

Each card contains:

- trigger
- watch output
- phone handoff moment

**Visual intent**
This is the main imagination section. The reader should see themselves using it.

---

### Section 7, Why phone handoff exists

**Layout**

- short title
- 2 short paragraphs
- highlighted line: `The watch is for awareness. The phone is for depth.`

**Visual intent**
This section removes the "why not do everything on the watch" objection.

---

### Section 8, Pricing

**Layout**
Single pricing card only.

Card contents:

- Personal
- $29/mo
- short audience line
- included bullets
- primary CTA

**Visual intent**
Keep this visually clean and unambiguous. Do not show hidden tiers.

---

### Section 9, Early access CTA

**Layout**

- short paragraph
- big CTA button

**Visual intent**
Repeat the core promise simply.

---

### Section 10, FAQ

**Layout**
4 short accordion items or simple stacked questions.

Questions:

- Why not just use the phone?
- Is this a full assistant on the watch?
- Why is phone handoff part of the product?
- Who is this for first?

## Recommended implementation note

If speed matters, build this page as:

- one clean hero
- one explanatory middle block
- one pricing card
- one CTA footer

The rest can stay lightweight and text-first.

## Key design rule

Do not make it feel like a broad consumer AI product.

It should feel like:

- focused
- technical
- crisp
- trustworthy
- operationally useful
