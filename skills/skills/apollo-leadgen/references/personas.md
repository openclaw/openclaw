# B2B Personas — Apollo Lead Generation

Persona definitions for Apollo.io People Search targeting. Each persona maps to a segment of VividWalls' commercial art buyer market.

Source: `~/.openclaw/workspace/businesses/vividwalls/marketing/persona-apollo-filters.json`

---

## 1. Hospitality Buyer

**Priority:** High — Hotels/resorts have highest AOV for wall art; recurring renovation cycles.

- **Titles:** Art Buyer, Procurement Manager, Design Director, Hotel Manager, Director of Purchasing, VP Design
- **Keywords:** hotel, hospitality, resort, restaurant group, boutique hotel, luxury hotel
- **Company Size:** 11-5,000 employees
- **Location:** United States

**Notes:** Luxury and boutique hotels are the sweet spot — they value unique art over generic prints. Target properties undergoing renovation or new openings for highest conversion.

---

## 2. Corporate Buyer

**Priority:** High — Office design budgets are recurring; large order potential.

- **Titles:** Office Manager, Facilities Manager, Workplace Experience, Head of Design, Facilities Director, Workplace Design Manager
- **Keywords:** corporate offices, coworking, commercial real estate, office design, workplace
- **Company Size:** 51-10,000 employees
- **Location:** United States

**Notes:** Coworking spaces and commercial real estate firms are particularly good targets. They outfit multiple locations and prefer unique art to differentiate their brand.

---

## 3. Healthcare Buyer

**Priority:** Medium — Large facilities with big budgets, but long sales cycles.

- **Titles:** Facilities Manager, Art Committee Chair, VP Patient Experience, Director of Facilities, Environmental Services Director
- **Keywords:** hospital, medical center, healthcare system, clinic, health network
- **Company Size:** 201-10,000 employees
- **Location:** United States

**Notes:** Healthcare systems increasingly invest in healing environments. Art committees make purchasing decisions — target the committee chair or patient experience leadership.

---

## 4. Retail Buyer

**Priority:** Medium — Visual merchandising needs are seasonal; good for campaign-timed outreach.

- **Titles:** Visual Merchandiser, Store Designer, Retail Design Director, Creative Director, Brand Experience Manager
- **Keywords:** retail chain, boutique retail, showroom, flagship store, retail design
- **Company Size:** 11-1,000 employees
- **Location:** United States

**Notes:** Best targeted ahead of seasonal refreshes (Q1 for spring, Q3 for holiday). Boutique retail and flagship stores value unique art to create experiential shopping environments.

---

## 5. Real Estate Stager

**Priority:** Lower — Smaller individual budgets but high volume; fast decision cycles.

- **Titles:** Home Stager, Staging Consultant, Real Estate Agent, Property Developer, Luxury Real Estate Agent
- **Keywords:** home staging, property staging, luxury real estate, real estate staging, model home
- **Company Size:** 1-200 employees
- **Location:** United States

**Notes:** Stagers are repeat buyers — once they find art they like, they order for multiple properties. Luxury segment stagers have higher budgets. Good entry point for building word-of-mouth in the trade.

---

## 6. Interior Designer

**Priority:** Medium — Multiplier effect; designers purchase for many clients.

- **Titles:** Interior Designer, Principal Designer, Design Director, Creative Director, Senior Designer
- **Keywords:** interior design firm, interior design studio, design studio, residential design
- **Company Size:** 1-200 employees
- **Location:** United States

**Notes:** Interior designers are the highest-value multiplier persona — one designer can drive purchases across dozens of client projects. Focus on principal/director-level designers who make procurement decisions. Trade program or designer discount can be a strong hook.
