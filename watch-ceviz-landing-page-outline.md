# Watch Ceviz, Landing Page Outline

## Goal

Turn the current product decisions into a one-page story that a founder-operator can understand quickly.

The page should answer:

- what is this
- who is it for
- why the watch matters
- why the phone is part of the product
- what the Personal plan includes
- why someone should join early access

## Section 1, Hero

### Goal

Make the product legible in under 5 seconds.

### Content

- Eyebrow: `For founder-operators`
- Headline: `Your agent workflows, one glance away.`
- Subheadline: `Trigger work, get a short trustworthy summary, and move to the phone only when approval or deeper context is needed.`
- Primary CTA: `Get early access`
- Secondary CTA: `See how it works`

## Section 2, What it is

### Goal

Prevent generic-assistant confusion.

### Content

Explain that Watch Ceviz is:

- not a chatbot on the wrist
- not a general assistant clone
- an access layer to important personal agent workflows

Suggested line:

- `Watch Ceviz gives technical operators fast awareness of important work without turning the watch into a tiny phone.`

## Section 3, How it works

### Goal

Show the watch -> phone -> backend model clearly.

### Content

#### Step 1

- Watch = trigger and glance

#### Step 2

- iPhone = depth, approval, recovery

#### Step 3

- OpenClaw = execution and orchestration

Suggested line:

- `The watch gives you the signal. The phone gives you the context. OpenClaw does the real work.`

## Section 4, Why this matters

### Goal

Tie the product to founder-operator pain.

### Content

Speak to people who:

- ship code themselves
- monitor deploys and incidents
- are away from the laptop often
- need fast situational clarity

Suggested bullets:

- `Check deploy risk before you open the laptop`
- `See whether an alert matters before you context-switch`
- `Get a PR snapshot without digging through tabs`

## Section 5, What you can do with Personal

### Goal

Make the entry plan concrete.

### Content

Title:

- `What Personal includes`

Bullets:

- active and recent jobs
- PR risk summary
- deploy status snapshot
- incident severity snapshot
- open-on-phone handoff
- structured phone report reading
- cancel / stop current job

Small note:

- `Built for fast awareness, not deep operator control.`

## Section 6, Example workflows

### Goal

Make the product demoable in the reader’s head.

### Content

#### Example 1, Deploy check

- ask from watch
- get short status summary
- open phone if risky

#### Example 2, PR snapshot

- ask from watch
- get one-sentence risk summary
- open phone for structured report

#### Example 3, Incident triage

- receive or request summary
- hear severity fast
- open phone for next context

## Section 7, Why the phone handoff exists

### Goal

Make the split feel intentional, not like a limitation.

### Content

Suggested line:

- `The watch is for fast awareness. The phone is for depth, approval, and recovery.`

Then add:

- this is a product decision, not a fallback
- depth moves to phone on purpose
- the watch stays fast by staying small

## Section 8, Pricing

### Goal

Make the public offer simple.

### Content

Plan card:

- `Personal`
- `$29/mo`
- `For founder-operators and solo technical builders`
- CTA: `Get early access`

Do not show publicly yet:

- Pro
- Team Pilot
- annual pricing

## Section 9, Early access CTA

### Goal

End with a clean ask.

### Content

Suggested line:

- `Join early access if you want faster awareness of deploys, PRs, incidents, and active jobs from your wrist.`

CTA:

- `Get early access`

## Section 10, Optional FAQ

### Goal

Handle obvious objections without adding clutter.

### Suggested FAQ items

- Why not just use the phone?
- Is this a full assistant on the watch?
- What kinds of workflows are supported?
- Why is the phone handoff part of the product?

1. Hero
2. What it is
3. How it works
4. Why it matters
5. What Personal includes
6. Example workflows

8. Pricing
9. Early access CTA
