# Command Center Guide

## What is the Command Center?

The Command Center is your main operating dashboard. Everything starts here.
It shows you what matters right now — priorities, schedule, system health,
and what needs your attention.

Think of it as your cockpit. You don't need to dig through menus or remember
commands. Just look at the dashboard and ask OpenClaw anything.

## What can you do here?

- **See priorities** — what needs attention today
- **Check the schedule** — upcoming events and deadlines for the next 10 hours
- **Review system health** — are all nodes online, are services running
- **Run daily workflows** — start-of-day routine, refresh widgets
- **Open brand dashboards** — jump into Full Digital or CUTMV
- **Review pending approvals** — actions waiting for your sign-off
- **Check overdue deadlines** — catch anything that slipped

## Common prompts

Try asking OpenClaw:

- "What should I focus on today?"
- "Run the start of day routine."
- "Show me what needs attention."
- "What's overdue?"
- "What needs my approval?"

## What requires approval?

- Executing budget changes
- Launching campaigns
- Infrastructure changes

Everything else — viewing data, running reports, checking status — is safe
and runs immediately.

## Panels

### Today Panel
Shows priorities, action items, and deadlines. This is the first thing
you should look at each morning.

### Schedule Panel
Your upcoming events and deadlines. Shows the next 10 hours by default.

### Brand KPI Chips
At-a-glance performance for Full Digital and CUTMV. Tap any chip for details.

### System Health
Quick status of your cluster. Green means everything is running smoothly.

### Pending Approvals
Actions waiting for your review. OpenClaw never executes sensitive changes
without your approval.

## How to start the day

1. Open the Command Center
2. Review the Today panel for priorities
3. Check the Brand KPI chips
4. Look at the schedule for the next 10 hours
5. Review any overdue deadlines
6. Press "Start the Day" to sync and refresh everything
7. Handle any pending approvals

That's it. You're caught up.
