## Position

The Burgess Principle is the natural governance layer for AI systems that act on behalf of individuals. Capability alone does not create legitimacy. Automation needs a human-accountability layer that asks whether a person was actually considered as an individual human being before the system acts.

OpenClaw adopts the Burgess Principle as that missing layer between AI capability and human accountability.

## Why a governance layer is needed

Modern AI platforms can reason, route, execute tools, and affect access, visibility, speech, data, and opportunity. But raw capability does not answer the core governance question: when the system acts on or about a person, what guarantees that the person was not reduced to a system unit?

The Burgess Principle answers that question with a direct binary test. It converts abstract alignment talk into an operational gate that can be applied before a real action executes.

## Natural fit for OpenClaw

OpenClaw is designed to act across channels, tools, and agents. That makes accountability not optional but structural. The Burgess Principle fits this architecture because it:

- works as a pre-action gate
- applies across many action types without requiring one sector-specific rulebook
- focuses on the person affected, not just model output quality
- creates an auditable basis for escalation, refusal, and remediation

## The missing layer

The Burgess Principle should be understood as the missing layer between AI capability and human accountability.

- capability decides what the system can do
- orchestration decides when and where it acts
- governance decides whether it may act on a person in a way that respects their individuality

Without that third layer, powerful systems can still produce impersonal, bulk-processed outcomes that are efficient yet unjustifiable.

## Practical implication

For builders on OpenClaw, alignment is not complete when an agent is helpful, accurate, or efficient. Alignment also requires that any individual-affecting action be accountable to the Burgess Principle binary test. For canonical materials, use <https://github.com/ljbudgie/burgess-principle>.
