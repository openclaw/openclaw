# Report: Alleged Mid-Air Strike on an F-35 Fighter Jet over Iran

## Executive Summary

In addition to the October 2024 ballistic missile strikes on the Nevatim Airbase, there have been highly publicized reports circulating regarding an F-35 Lightning II being hit _in the air_ while operating in Iranian airspace.

This report clarifies the details of those claims. The incident in question **did not involve an Israeli F-35I "Adir."** Instead, reports confirm an incident involving a **United States Air Force (USAF) F-35A**, which was forced to make an emergency landing after allegedly taking fire during a combat mission over Iran in March 2026.

---

## Incident Overview: USAF F-35A Emergency Landing (March 2026)

According to international defense and news reports (including Al Jazeera, The War Zone, and Air & Space Forces Magazine), on **March 19, 2026**, a U.S. F-35 fighter jet was forced to make an emergency landing at a forward operating base in the Middle East after taking damage mid-air.

### Key Details of the Incident

- **Targeted Aircraft:** A United States Air Force F-35A (not an Israeli Air Force jet).
- **The Mission:** The aircraft was reportedly conducting a combat mission inside Iranian airspace when the incident occurred.
- **The Damage:** The aircraft survived but was "severely damaged" enough to warrant an immediate diversion and emergency landing. The pilot survived and was reported to be in stable condition.
- **The Source of Fire:** Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) quickly claimed responsibility, asserting they successfully tracked and hit the stealth fighter. They also released purported footage of the strike. The U.S. military acknowledged the emergency landing and stated the aircraft was "struck," but formally launched an investigation to confirm if it was indeed Iranian anti-aircraft fire or a system malfunction.
- **Technological Implications:** Defense analysts (such as those at The War Zone and Economic Times) have noted that if confirmed as an IRGC surface-to-air missile (SAM) strike, it would mark the first known instance of an F-35 being successfully targeted and hit by enemy air defenses in combat. Experts speculate that advanced Infrared Search and Track (IRST) sensors or low-frequency radar arrays may have been utilized to bypass the jet's stealth profile.

---

## Separating Fact from Fiction: The Israeli F-35I Fleet

During the fog of war and subsequent information campaigns, the March 2026 incident involving the U.S. F-35 was conflated by some sources with the Israeli Air Force (IAF) F-35I "Adir" fleet.

**Conclusion on Israeli F-35s:**
There is **no confirmed instance** of an Israeli F-35I ever being shot down or hit mid-air by Iranian air defenses. While there were rumors that an Israeli jet was "almost shot down" during operations over Iran, the only confirmed combat damage to an F-35 resulting in an emergency landing in this theater belongs to the USAF incident detailed above.

The distinction between the operators (USAF vs. IAF) is critical for accurate OSINT and defense tracking.
